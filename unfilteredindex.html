Mission Unfiltered

The Unfiltered Project aims to empower citizens by making the elusive information on water quality assessments of their local water system more accessible by streamlining the processing of EPA data into one location, as well as decoding the policy jargon from the government reports into clear, transparent language.

Decoding the Data

Government reports on public drinking water test results are scattered among many national, state and district-level datasets. Our team pieced together data from across these platforms so users can uncover information about the water standards from wherever they are located across the United States.

Learning from Experience

Our team's interest in the issue of water contamination hits close to home. As a native of Flint, Michigan, our team lead witnessed the devastation that government failure on water safety compliance matters can have on a community.

Improving Accessibility

Our team created the Unfiltered service so it can be used. In order to make the service more accessible to a variety of users across the U.S., we made sure the platform can be utilized on a mobile application, as well as on web browser.

Empowering People

Our team believes that citizens should be able to access public information on the safety of their drinking water easily. Our service provides citizens access to the results of the water quality test results of their current location immediately, as well as to the contact information of their local Department of Environmental Protection representative.

Lead Poisoning

While no amount of lead exposure is safe, the Center for Disease Control has capped the amount of lead contamination public drinking water may contain before they consider it a health hazard at 15 parts per billion.

In 2015, The amount of lead found in Flint in the fall of 2015 was over 100 parts per billion.
